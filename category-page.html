What Is Insomnia?

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that affects millions of individuals across the globe. People with Insomnia might struggle to fall asleep or stay asleep. Adults need a minimum of seven hours of sleep every day, and it can be up to nine hours. However, people in the US encounter insomnia frequently.

People who have acute Insomnia will experience health hazards like exhaustive feelings, loss of concentration, and more. Chronic Insomnia can be the root cause of various serious health conditions. Chronic diseases such as depression, cardiovascular disease, diabetes are the result of missing the regular sleep cycle. Usually, people who have Insomnia can be lazy, feel sick mentally and physically, and mood swings.

Types of Insomnia

Classification based on the cause

There are two types of Insomnia, namely primary and secondary Insomnia. People who have primary Insomnia don't have their sleep disorder connected with underlying health conditions. However, secondary Insomnia is linked with a health condition like asthma, cancer, heartburn, and more.

Classification based on the duration

The sleep disorder is classified as acute (short-term) or chronic (long-term). In some patients, the sleep disorder affects people frequently and goes away without any medications. If a person has acute Insomnia, the duration can be measured from one day to a few weeks. For people who have chronic Insomnia, the sleep disorder may affect as long as three months. People need to be safe by taking proper sleep aid medications for a sleep disorder.

Classification based on the severity

If you have mild Insomnia, it is due to a lack of sleep and exhaustive feeling. Moderate Insomnia can affect your daily life, whereas severe Insomnia can have a serious impact on your health conditions. There are diverse factors considered to determine the type of Insomnia.

How to prevent Insomnia?

If you want to prevent Insomnia, it is essential to have good sleep practices, aka sleep hygiene. Firstly, have the proper timing to sleep and wake up. If you sleep at 22.00 hours and wake up at 5 or 6 in the morning, try to maintain the same timing every day. The sleep cycles can be disturbed if you take naps in the daytime. Try to stop taking naps in the day and sleep simultaneously to get used to the timing.

  • You should avoid using phones before bed as the lights can hinder your sleep cycle.
  • It is essential to skip caffeine, alcohol, and nicotine, keeping you awake for a long time. In addition, people who drink alcohol have troubled sleep leading to lower sleep quality.
  • Ensure that you indulge in regular physical activities, but you shouldn't work out before bedtime. It can hinder your sleep cycle so work out three hours before you go to bed.
  • Avoid heavy meals during the night and have a few snacks before going to bed. Also, it might be hard to sleep without eating, so ensure you have some food before sleep.
  • It will be easier to fall asleep if the room is dark and cool. A quiet room is all you need to sleep peacefully. If you have been finding it hard to sleep due to noise problems, use earplugs.
  • You can listen to music or read a book before you go to bed. It can be soothing and relaxes your mind to sleep peacefully.

Cure Insomnia without sleeping pills

If you have questions like how to cure Insomnia, it is essential to know the best treatments. To cure Insomnia, you can try cognitive behavioral therapy as it is found effective. Behavioral changes can surely impact sleep without medications.

Also, you can try the above steps like avoiding coffee and alcohol before bed, listening to music, reading a book, shutting down phones at least an hour before bedtime, and a good sleep schedule. Whether you want to prevent or find the cure for Insomnia, the above steps can be effective. So, start now and ensure that you tag along with the instruction to sleep peacefully.
